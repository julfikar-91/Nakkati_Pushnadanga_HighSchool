Our Legacy

A Journey of Seven Decades

From a remote village with no road access to a beacon of secondary education for the entire Cooch Behar region.

In the early 1950s, the villages of Nakkati, Pushnadanga, and Haribhanga in Cooch Behar-I Block were isolated — home to scheduled caste and minority communities — with virtually no road infrastructure.

Students who wished to pursue secondary education faced a 14-kilometre daily walk to Coochbehar Sadar or Dinhata sub-division, making schooling nearly inaccessible for these communities.

Recognising this critical gap, community leaders joined forces in 1953 to establish Nakkati Pushnadanga High School — bringing quality education right to the doorstep of the community.

Today, the school stands as a thriving institution with over 1,238 students, 29 dedicated faculty, a library, and a 100% board pass rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What You Looking For?

What classes does the school offer?
The school offers Classes V through XII, covering Upper Primary (V–VIII), Secondary / Madhyamik (IX–X), and Higher Secondary (XI–XII) in both Arts and Science streams.
Which boards is the school affiliated with?
The school is affiliated with WBBSE (West Bengal Board of Secondary Education) for Classes IX–X (Madhyamik) and WBCHSE (West Bengal Council of Higher Secondary Education) for Classes XI–XII.
Is there technical support / office available?
The school office is open Monday–Friday 10:30 AM–3:30 PM and Saturday 10:30 AM–1:30 PM. You can also visit the Banglar Shiksha portal at school.banglarshiksha.gov.in for online enquiries.
How do I get admission to the school?
Admission forms are available at the school office during office hours. New session admissions typically open in June–July each year. Visit or contact the school office for the latest schedule.
Is the school government sponsored?
Yes. Nakkati Pushnadanga High School is a government-sponsored co-educational institution managed by the Department of Education, Government of West Bengal. UDISE Code: 19030809101.

How to Reach Us
By Road: Cooch Behar is connected via NH27. School is ~1 km south of town via Haribhanga Road.
By Rail: Cooch Behar Junction (NF Railway) is the nearest station, then local transport to Haribhanga.
By Bus: Local buses and auto-rickshaws run regularly from Cooch Behar town to Pushnadanga / Haribhanga.
