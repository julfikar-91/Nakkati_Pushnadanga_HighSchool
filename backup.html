About Our School

Shaping Futures for
Over Seven Decades

Nakkati Pushnadanga High School is a co-educational, state-government institution nestled in Haribhanga village, Cooch Behar-I Block. Founded in 1953, it has been a transformative force for scheduled caste and minority communities across this region.

With Bengali as the primary medium and English as the secondary, we offer Classes V through XII — affiliated with both WBBSE for Madhyamik and WBCHSE for Higher Secondary examinations.

Our Legacy

A Journey of Seven Decades

From a remote village with no road access to a beacon of secondary education for the entire Cooch Behar region.

In the early 1950s, the villages of Nakkati, Pushnadanga, and Haribhanga in Cooch Behar-I Block were isolated — home to scheduled caste and minority communities — with virtually no road infrastructure. Walking was the only mode of travel.

Students who wished to pursue secondary education faced a 14-kilometre daily walk to Coochbehar Sadar or Dinhata sub-division — making secondary schooling almost inaccessible for the youth of these villages.

Recognising this critical educational gap, community leaders and visionary local educators joined forces in 1953 to establish Nakkati Pushnadanga High School — bringing quality education right to the doorstep of the community.

Today, the school stands as a thriving institution with over 1,238 students, 29 dedicated faculty, a library, and a 100% board examination pass rate — a testament to over seven decades of unwavering commitment.

How to Reach Us

By Road: Cooch Behar is connected via NH27. School is ~1 km south of town via Haribhanga Road.

By Rail: Cooch Behar Junction (NF Railway) is the nearest station, then local transport to Haribhanga.

By Bus: Local buses and auto-rickshaws run regularly from Cooch Behar town to Pushnadanga / Haribhanga.
